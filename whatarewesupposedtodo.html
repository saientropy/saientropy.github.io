After spending 3 years in the US, I picked up a habit of keeping back the shopping cart in its rightful place after loading my stuff in my car. Its just nice, respectful and it feels good after doing so.

Today, I was in my hometown in India, and I was doing the same, and my dad yelled at me not to keep the cart back becauase there was a person who job was to keep all the carts back. I was a bit surprised, not because there was a person to keep the cart back, but because my dad yelled. When I asked him why, he told me and I could not believe this " If everyone keeps keeping the cart back, that person will lose his job"

People hate losing jobs, but I don't think humans are supposed to keep them fucking shopping carts back in their place and obviously people do jobs that are boring, hateful and despicable for money and survival-based plans for their existance.

The question arises, whether or not there will be a bad displacements in labor becauase of AI, what if we figure out a way to give people some kind og UBI. The reason its not just straight-up UBI is because that money cannot be all-cash. It has to take care of basic living: food, clothes, rent / house, medicines, child-care, medical expenses.

This seems like in one way or another this will fail, mainly I thing through misallocation of resources, systemic problems, but also because, giving something like a UBI needs a new system to be built to support it: A utopian democracy with incentives aligned with no globalism.

That still does not answer the question, what the fuck are we supposed to do, is it just jobs, one after another?

I dont think so, I think its gonna be this: basically every person gets immense courage to go do whatever they want in life (questioning their passions and following their talents) and in fact we will reach a point where we will need more humans because humans will just keep making the world better and better. The world will be far from dystopia.

I have no clue when this might happen, but I pray, its soon enough. It starts from me.
